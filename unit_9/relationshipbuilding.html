Relationship-building Questionnaire

Use the questionnaire below to gauge the strength of your relationship with each of your employees. The way you use this questionnaire is dependent upon the number of employees you have. If you have a smaller number of employees, you can answer the questions in the questionnaire for each individual. If you have a high number of employees, you can have the head of each of your departments or the lead on each of your committees to complete the questionnaire as pertained to the employees in their respective groups.

  • Name of employee: Position title:
  • How would you characterize your relationship with this employee? Explain.
  • What is the nature of conversation this employee has with you? Is it always work-related or is it at times informal? Does this employee ever approach you face-to-face or only through email? Explain.
  • How would you characterize your interactions with this employee? Tense? Relaxed? Rushed? Other? Explain.
  • What emotions does this employee evoke? Anxiety? Frustration? Calmness? Other? How about the employee? What emotions do you invoke out of the employee? Fear? Anxiety? Aggressiveness? Calmness? Other? Why do you think that is?
  • How often do you greet the employee and chat whether by phone, email or in-person? Do you feel like you should do this more often?
  • How often do you praise the employee? How often do you criticize the employee? Do you differentiate between the type of language you use for praise from the language you use for criticizing or is one type of language much more harsh than the other? What effect does this have on your employee?
  • How often does your employee approach you for help or does your employee typically approach co-workers for help or extra clarification? How do you react to your employee when you are approached for help?
